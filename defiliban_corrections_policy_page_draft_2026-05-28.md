# Corrections Policy

DeFiliban aims to publish accurate reporting, analysis, and research-driven explainers. When factual issues appear in published content, they should be corrected clearly and responsibly.

This page explains how DeFiliban approaches corrections, clarifications, and updates.

Last updated: May 28, 2026

## Our Standard

If a published article contains a meaningful factual error, the goal is to correct it as promptly as possible once the issue is verified.

If new information materially changes the understanding of a story, the article should be updated so readers are not left with outdated or misleading information.

## Corrections, Clarifications, and Updates

DeFiliban treats these actions as distinct:

- a correction fixes a factual inaccuracy
- a clarification improves wording or context where the original meaning could mislead readers
- an update adds new verified information to a developing story

Not every revision requires a formal correction note, but meaningful factual fixes should not be made in a way that hides the substance of the change.

## How Corrections Should Be Handled

When a substantive factual correction is needed, DeFiliban aims to:

- correct the inaccurate information in the article
- avoid leaving incorrect material standing without clarification
- add context when needed so readers can understand the change

Where appropriate, a note may be added to explain that a correction or significant update has been made.

As a working standard, routine correction notes can appear at the bottom of an article, while major corrections that materially affect the core understanding of a story may be noted more prominently.

## Minor Edits

Minor edits such as grammar, formatting, punctuation, or small readability improvements do not usually require a correction note if they do not change the factual meaning of the article.

Routine updates to developing stories also do not always require a formal correction note when they add new verified information rather than fix a factual inaccuracy.

## Developing Stories

Crypto and DeFi stories often evolve quickly. Early reports may require follow-up updates as protocols, exchanges, issuers, regulators, or public filings release more complete information.

In those cases, DeFiliban aims to update coverage in a way that improves accuracy rather than leaving readers with stale framing from an earlier stage of the story.

## Reader Reports

If you believe a DeFiliban article contains a factual error, missing context, or unclear wording, you can contact us through our [Contact](https://defiliban.io/contact/) page.

When reporting an issue, it is most useful to include:

- the article URL
- the sentence or claim in question
- the reason you believe it is inaccurate or unclear
- a source, document, or reference if available

If DeFiliban establishes a dedicated editorial or corrections email in the future, that contact route should be added here as well.

## Scope

This policy applies to editorial content published by DeFiliban, including news, analysis, and research-style explainers.

Sponsored, partner, or promotional material should also be corrected when it contains factual inaccuracies, but it should remain clearly distinguishable from independent editorial coverage.
